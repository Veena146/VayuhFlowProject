About us

At Vayuh, we’re on a mission to empower individuals and communities to take control of our planet’s future. than just a platform, Vayuh is a transformative movement that enables you to contribute and validate high-quality climate data. This data fuels cutting-edge Climate AI models designed to tackle the most pressing environmental challenges of our time.

Our journey began in 2018, with a focus on developing low-cost indoor air quality monitors. Over time, we evolved to leverage that data in building AI models, driving deeper insights and innovative solutions for a sustainable world.

We understand that the power of an AI model lies in the strength of its training data. To build remarkable AI, one must begin with remarkable data. That’s why we prioritize collaboration and innovation, ensuring the data we pool is accurate, impactful, and transformative.

Frequently asked
questions

What is Vayuh?

Vayuh is a blockchain and AI driven climate data network that incentivizes data contributors and validators for participating in building the Vayuh ecosystem.

How can I participate in the Vayuh ecosystem?

You can participate in several ways.

  • 1. Contribute, by feeding IOT and Sensor data for various climate parameters like air quality, temperature, humidity etc. directly to our network using the Vayuh app.
  • 2. Contribute, peer reviewed data from your research to our ecosystem.
  • 3. Validate, by establishing yourself as a trusted partner you can earn rewards for peer reviewing data.
  • 4. Develop AI analytical models to better interpret the data.

How do the AI models work?

In the past 7 years, since the inception of Vayuh, we have built several AI models that help process climate data. Our AI models can automatically review the incoming data and look for inconsistencies and errors. The models look for missing values, historical trend comparisons, confidence checks to assign a quality score to the data set. These data sets are then used to train other AI models that work in conjunction with various EPA models like AERMOD, CMAQ etc. and other peer reviewed models in research stages.
